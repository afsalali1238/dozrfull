Dozr brand system — addendum

The dot.

A single yellow full stop, used as a standalone mark wherever the wordmark is impractical or unnecessary — the equivalent of Amazon's smile. This is a supplement to Dozr_Brand_Guidelines.html: fold it into section on brand marks the next time that file is regenerated.

The rule

Why a period, not a comma

A comma implies a pause. Dozr's pitch is decisiveness — book it, dispatch it, done. The full stop can also stand alone as a mark (favicon, avatar, loader) without the wordmark next to it, which a comma reads oddly doing.

Construction

Solid circle, #FFC400, no outline, no gradient, no tint. Default diameter sits at roughly 0.4× the cap-height of whatever text it accompanies — 8px next to 11px mono labels, scale proportionally elsewhere. Minimum clear space equal to its own diameter on all sides.

Do / don't

Use
Solid #FFC400, never tinted or outlinedDo
One consistent size relationship to adjacent text — never scaled for effectDo
At the start or end of something (a label, a sentence, a status) — never mid-decorationDo
As a repeating pattern, wallpaper, or divider fillerDon't
In any color other than brand yellowDon't
Combined with the pictorial dozr mark (nav/footer logo) in the same lockup — the dot is a separate, lighter-weight device, not a replacement for the primary markDon't

Relationship to the primary mark

The existing pictorial mark (the yellow icon in favicon.svg and the site nav/footer logo) is the primary logomark and stays as-is — this dot is a secondary, more minimal device for places a full logo doesn't fit: punctuation, status, bullets, avatars. Treat them as two tools, not competitors.
