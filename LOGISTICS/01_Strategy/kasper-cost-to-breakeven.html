Six scenarios worth stress-testing before committing capital

01Platform cost lands above AED 5.50/deviceEvery AED 1 shift in Wialon/Navixy pricing moves break-even by ~11 devices. Still unconfirmed.

03b — Technical & operational scenarios (CTO lens)

What can go wrong on the hardware, network, and platform side

The financial model assumes every device installs cleanly, stays connected, and keeps working for 12+ months. As CTO, here's what actually threatens that assumption — none of these show up in the P&L until they happen.

#ScenarioRisk
01Hardware supply chain / lead timeTeltonika units are ordered in MOQ-50 batches to hold landed cost — a supply delay stalls the ramp directly; single-vendor dependency with no backup source qualified.
02du single-network coverage gapsConstruction sites are exactly where dead zones happen. Every device that needs Onomondo's multi-network failover costs AED 20/mo instead of AED 8.33–10 — a 2–2.4× SIM cost increase on affected units.
032G/3G network sunsetUAE carriers are phasing down legacy bands. Confirm FMC130's supported bands cover du/e&'s 5-year roadmap — a mid-life forced hardware swap would wipe out Year 2+ margins on affected devices.
04No OTA update infrastructure yetFirmware bugs or config changes at 400+ devices need remote push capability. Without it, every fix means a truck roll — reinstall-level cost on a "recurring" opex line.
05Device failure / DOA rate not modeledNo warranty-replacement buffer stock or failure-rate assumption exists in the financial model. Even a 3–5% annual failure rate on 424 devices is 13–21 replacement units/year — AED 5,300–8,600 in unbudgeted hardware.
06Installation quality controlNo certified-installer program yet. A bad install (loose OBD connection, wrong wiring on CAN adapters) causes intermittent data or false readings — drives support tickets and reinstall visits that aren't priced in.
07Wialon vs custom-build timing riskIf the Wialon quote comes back high and custom build is chosen, the 4–6 month dev timeline delays activation of every device queued behind it — direct hit to the ramp schedule, not just a cost line.
08Data security & residencyLive location + engine-hour data on client equipment is sensitive. If custom-built and hosted outside UAE (DigitalOcean/AWS region choice), confirm this doesn't violate client data-residency expectations before scaling past pilot.
09Backend scalability untestedNo load test yet for Codec 8 Extended ingestion at 400–500 concurrent devices. AWS/DigitalOcean sizing in the feasibility study is an estimate, not a benchmark — confirm before the fleet outgrows a single small instance.
10Device theft / tamper on unattended equipmentGPS units on parked construction equipment are a known theft target. No tamper-alert or anti-theft mounting spec defined yet — theft losses aren't in the capex-replacement model.
11Support team doesn't scale linearlyAED 16,000/month salary line implies a very small team. Ticket volume (coverage complaints, install issues, billing questions) tends to scale faster than headcount at this budget past ~200–300 active devices.
12Third-party platform lock-inIf launching on Wialon/Navixy, confirm data export rights and contract terms up front — switching to custom-build later is much harder if historical tracking data isn't portable.
Highest-priority technical unknowns to close before scaling past the pilot batch: (1) confirmed failure/warranty-replacement rate from Teltonika, (2) a load test of the ingestion pipeline at realistic Tier-3 CAN-data volumes, and (3) a certified-installer process — these three are cheap to fix at 10–50 devices and expensive to fix at 400+.

06 — Bottom line
◈
Recommendation
Raise AED 120,000–150,000, not AED 226,000

Deploying the 424-device mid-tier break-even fleet costs AED 225,992 in hardware, installation, and SIM activation — but annual upfront billing means clients fund most of that themselves on Day 1. The actual cash Kasper needs on hand is the peak of the funding gap between fixed overhead and the ramp: AED 92,479 at Month 13, before renewals start layering in. A raise of AED 120,000–150,000 covers that trough with a working margin of safety, without over-capitalising a business that's designed to fund itself.
