№GUIDEThe Business
What we are building

GPS-as-a-Service — lease devices, charge monthly

Kasper installs GPS tracking devices on vendor vehicles and equipment. Vendors pay a monthly subscription. Kasper owns the hardware. Clients pay annually upfront. The device data feeds a future intelligence layer worth significantly more than the subscription revenue.

AED 60Tier 1 — basic tracking / device / mo
AED 80Tier 2 — fleet analytics / device / mo
AED 110Tier 3 — heavy equipment / device / mo
424Break-even device count (base case)
Month 13Break-even timeline — 70/20/10 mix
66–84%Year 2 gross margin across tiers
Why Year 1 margins look low — and why that's fine: In Year 1, hardware and installation cost is spread across 12 monthly payments. This pulls the gross margin down to 19–26% for the first 12 months. From Year 2 onwards, hardware cost drops to zero — the device is already installed and paid for — and margins jump to 66–84%. The business is designed this way deliberately. Annual upfront billing means the hardware is already recovered in cash on Day 1, even though it's amortised in the P&L.
Why now: Kasper already dispatches vendors manually — real relationships, real operations. GPS is the natural next layer. The first 10–25 devices go onto vendors already working with Kasper today. Near-zero customer acquisition cost for the initial fleet. Every device installed also builds the data asset that powers the future telematics intelligence products. Year 1 gross margin is 19–26% (hardware amortised). Year 2 margin is 66–84% as hardware cost drops to zero.

№13 / 15Investment Ask
How much capital actually needs to be in the bank

Peak funding need: ~AED 92,000 — recommend raising AED 120,000–150,000

Total deployment capex for 424 mid-tier devices is AED 225,992 (previous panel) — but that figure overstates the real ask, because each device brings in AED 1,060 upfront against AED 533 of cost. What matters is the cumulative cash position month by month: fixed costs (AED 20,333/month) plus recurring per-device opex run continuously, while renewal cash only starts arriving at Month 13, once the first cohort's annual contracts come up again.

−AED 92,479Peak cumulative cash deficit — Month 13, just before renewals begin
Month 20Cumulative cash turns positive (base ramp, 85% renewal rate)
Month 24Cumulative position: +AED 46,000 and compounding

Model: 10 devices Month 1, +35/month thereafter, 100% Tier 2, AED 20,333/month fixed costs, 85% renewal rate per cohort at Month 13. This is a cash-flow simulation, not the accrual break-even used elsewhere in this study — it's the number a bank balance actually needs to survive, not the point where monthly revenue crosses monthly fixed cost on paper.

Funding scenarioAmountCovers
Bare minimum (peak deficit only)AED 92,500Exact cash trough, zero margin for a slower ramp or lower renewal rate

Why not raise the full AED 226K deployment capex: that would sit as idle cash for most of the ramp — annual-upfront billing means Kasper is never actually AED 226K out of pocket at any single point, only ~AED 92K at the worst point. Raising more than ~AED 150K without a specific reason is over-capitalising a business designed to be self-funding.
