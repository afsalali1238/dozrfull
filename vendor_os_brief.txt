Section 1 — What We Are Building
The product, the users, the problem we solve
The one-line pitch
Kasper Vendor OS
A mobile-first SaaS portal that lets UAE logistics and equipment rental SMEs receive bookings via WhatsApp, send professional quotes in 60 seconds, dispatch drivers with GPS tracking, collect digital proof of delivery, and get paid — all without leaving their phone.
The problem we are solving
The UAE has thousands of small fleet operators — companies with 1 to 50 trucks, cranes, or pieces of heavy equipment. They process hundreds of thousands of AED in jobs every month. But their entire operation runs on WhatsApp voice notes, shared Excel files, and handwritten paper delivery notes.
Pain point
What happens today vs. what Kasper Vendor OS does
Quoting
Today: 30–60 mins of back-and-forth WhatsApp messages, manual Excel price calc, PDF emailed from phone. Vendor OS: Type a price, tap Send. Professional VAT-compliant PDF sent in 10 seconds.
Driver dispatch
Today: Phone call + WhatsApp voice note. No confirmation. Driver forgets the address. Vendor OS: Tap assign driver → driver gets WhatsApp with deep link to full job card.
Live tracking
Today: Client calls "where is my truck?" Driver calls back with a verbal location. Vendor OS: Client has live GPS map link. Driver phone pushes location every 10 seconds.
Proof of delivery
Today: Paper delivery note. Often lost, disputed, or never signed. Invoice delayed 30 days. Vendor OS: Driver photos cargo, client signs on phone. ePOD generated instantly. Invoice auto-sent.
Invoicing & payment
Today: Word doc invoice emailed. No payment link. Chased by phone 30–60 days later. Vendor OS: Invoice + Telr payment link auto-sent on ePOD completion. Target: 5-day collection.
Cash flow visibility
Today: Owner has no idea what is outstanding until they check WhatsApp and count manually. Vendor OS: Dashboard shows revenue, outstanding invoices, and on-time rate in real time.
The three users — their experience must be designed separately
Every job in Kasper Vendor OS involves exactly three people. Each has a completely different relationship with technology and a different level of digital literacy.

Section 2 — The Complete Job Lifecycle
Every step from first WhatsApp to final payment, with who does what
Phase 1 — Enquiry capture
1
Client sends enquiry
Client scans vendor's QR code or taps WhatsApp link (wa.me pre-fill). Message includes: vehicle type, date, pickup, destination, cargo, name, phone.
2
System captures it
WhatsApp webhook (360dialog) fires → Supabase function creates job record with status="enquiry". job_code auto-generated: KSP-XXXX.
3
Client gets confirmation
Auto-reply: "Your enquiry KSP-0042 is received. You'll get a quote within 2 hours." Sent via WhatsApp API.
4
Vendor gets notified
WhatsApp notification to vendor: "New enquiry from Emirates Steel — 40ft flatbed, Khor Fakkan → Dubai, Apr 30. Tap to view."
5
Fallback (Phase 1 MVP)
If API not ready: vendor manually enters enquiry in portal. wa.me deep link still sends client the confirmation.
Phase 2 — Quoting
1
Vendor opens portal
Taps notification → opens vendor.html PWA (bookmarked on home screen). Sees new RFQ at top of list.
2
Vendor prices the job
One input: "Your Price (AED)". Optional: notes. Taps "Send Quote".
3
PDF generated
jsPDF generates quote PDF client-side: job details, price ex-VAT, 5% VAT, total inc-VAT, vendor TRN, 48h validity, payment terms.
4
Quote delivered
PDF uploaded to Supabase Storage. WhatsApp API sends client: PDF link + "Tap Approve or Decline". Email copy via Resend.
5
Client receives quote
Opens WhatsApp. Taps PDF link. Reviews. Taps "✓ Approve" or "✗ Decline" button (links to approve.html or decline.html).
Phase 3 — Confirmation & dispatch
1
Client approves
approve.html loads with job_code from URL. Client taps confirm. Supabase updates status="accepted".
2
PO generated
System auto-generates PO PDF: all job details, agreed price, vendor bank details, reference number. Sent to both client and vendor via WhatsApp.
3
Vendor assigns driver
Vendor sees PO in Active Jobs tab. Taps "Assign Driver". Selects from their fleet roster. Confirms.
4
Driver notified
WhatsApp sent to driver: "New job KSP-0042. Tap to open." Link: driver.html?job=KSP-0042. No password. No app download.
5
Job status updates
Supabase status → "assigned". Vendor dashboard updates. Client gets WhatsApp: "Your driver Mohammed is assigned. Track live: [link]"
Phase 4 — Execution & delivery
1
Driver opens job
Taps WhatsApp link → driver.html loads with full job card pre-populated. No typing. No login.
2
Driver starts trip
Taps "Start Trip" → browser GPS activates → location pushed to Supabase every 10 seconds via REST API.
3
Client tracks live
Client's tracking link (track.html?job=KSP-0042) shows Leaflet map with live driver pin. Updates every 10s. Shows ETA.
4
GPS degrades gracefully
If signal lost >5 mins: amber banner shows "GPS lost – last seen 6 mins ago." Driver can type manual location update.
5
Delivery confirmed
Driver taps "Mark Delivered". Takes photo of cargo. Supabase Storage uploads image. Status → "delivered".
Phase 5 — ePOD & payment
1
Driver signs ePOD
Canvas signature pad on driver.html. Driver signs with finger. Timestamp + GPS coordinates captured.
2
Client signs ePOD
Client receives WhatsApp link. Opens track.html. Signs on their device. "Accepted by [client name] at [time]" captured.
3
Invoice auto-generated
jsPDF creates invoice: references PO number, delivery note ID, VAT-compliant line items, Telr payment link embedded.
4
Invoice delivered
WhatsApp + email to client. Status → "invoiced". Vendor dashboard shows outstanding amount.
5
Payment collected
Client taps Telr link. Pays by card or Apple Pay. Telr webhook fires → Supabase status → "paid". Vendor receipt sent.
6
LPO / bank transfer alt
Client uploads LPO in portal. Triggers 30-day reminder cycle. Or vendor marks manual bank transfer received.

Section 5 — Access, Auth & Security
How each user type gets in, and how data stays isolated
Vendor authentication — full Supabase Auth
Vendors are real SaaS users. They have accounts, sessions, and their data is completely isolated from other vendors at the database level.
1
Sign up
Vendor visits vendor.html → redirected to onboard.html. Enters phone number → Supabase sends OTP via SMS → enters OTP → authenticated. auth.users record created. vendor_id = auth.uid().
2
First-time setup
onboard.html wizard: company name, TRN, logo upload, bank details, add first 3 drivers. Completion creates vendors table row.
3
Login (returning)
Phone OTP again. Session stored in localStorage. Supabase manages token refresh. Session valid 7 days. Phone re-OTP if expired.
4
Session check
Every vendor page calls requireVendorAuth() on load. If no valid session → redirect to login. No page loads without auth.
5
RLS enforcement
Every Supabase query automatically filters by vendor_id = auth.uid(). Vendor A cannot read Vendor B's data even with a direct REST call.
6
Logout
Clears Supabase session. Clears localStorage. Redirects to index.html.
Driver access — zero-friction link auth
Drivers must not be blocked by passwords. They get a pre-authenticated WhatsApp link. The link IS the authentication.
1
Link generated
When vendor assigns driver, system generates: driver.html?job=KSP-0042&t=[signed_token]
2
Token contents
Supabase Edge Function generates a signed JWT containing: job_id, vendor_id, driver_id, expires_in: 24h.
3
Page load
driver.html extracts job code and token from URL. Calls Supabase Edge Function to validate token. If valid, loads job data.
4
What driver can do
Read their job details. Push GPS location. Upload delivery photo. Sign ePOD. Mark delivered. NOTHING else.
5
What driver cannot do
Read other jobs. Read other vendor data. Change prices. Access any other page with their token.
6
Token expiry
After 24 hours, the link shows "This link has expired. Contact your employer for a new link." Vendor regenerates from portal.
Client access — job code URL auth (read-only)
Clients have no account. Their "authentication" is knowing the job code. This is appropriate for read-only tracking and signing.
1
approve.html
URL: approve.html?job=KSP-0042&sig=[hash]. Client taps WhatsApp link. Page loads job details. Approve / Decline buttons.
2
track.html
URL: track.html?job=KSP-0042. No secret needed — tracking is intentionally public for the job code holder.
3
RLS for public access
Supabase policy: jobs SELECT allowed WHERE job_code = [param] AND (status != 'draft'). No auth token required for read-only tracking fields.
4
ePOD signing
Client signature on track.html is POST only (no read beyond their job). Token in URL prevents CSRF. Stored as base64 in jobs table.
5
What client cannot do
See prices (quoted_price and vendor_price are excluded from public SELECT policy). See other jobs. Modify any field except their own signature.
Kasper ops access — internal admin
ops.html is the internal Kasper dashboard. Only accessible with a special Kasper admin role set in Supabase Auth metadata.
Feature in ops.html
Description
All vendors view
See every vendor company, their plan, job count, last active date. Activate / deactivate.
All jobs view
Filter by date, status, vendor, service type. Override any job status. Download any document.
RFQ assignment
When a new enterprise client job comes in, ops selects 3–5 vendors and sends them the RFQ. Sets their prices and deadlines.
Vendor onboarding
Approve pending vendor registrations. Set plan tier. Trigger welcome WhatsApp.
Billing overview
See MRR, churn, overdue accounts. (Phase 2: Stripe integration for billing).
Section 6 — Antigravity Build Guide
How to set up the agent, what prompts to use, in what order
What Antigravity is and why it's perfect for this project
Antigravity is Google's new agent-first IDE (released November 2025, free in preview). It is a VS Code fork where AI agents autonomously plan, write code, run terminal commands, and test in the browser. You give it a mission. It builds across multiple files without you clicking through every step.
It connects to: your file system (creates/edits files), your terminal (runs npm commands, starts servers), and your browser (tests pages, validates output). For Kasper Vendor OS — which is vanilla JS with no build step — this is ideal. No Webpack config to debug. No framework abstraction. The agent writes HTML, runs a local server, opens the page in the browser, tests it, and iterates.
Setup steps before running any agent
1
Install Antigravity
Download from antigravity.google/download. Free with personal Google account. Install on Mac, Windows, or Linux.
2
Open the project
File → Open Folder → select kasper-vendor-os/ (this repo). All files visible in Explorer panel.
3
Read the AGENTS.md
Antigravity auto-reads AGENTS.md in the project root. This file tells agents your conventions, stack, and forbidden patterns.
4
Set up .env
Copy .env.example to .env. Fill in your Supabase URL and anon key from supabase.co. Do NOT fill in paid API keys yet — Phase 1 uses free tools only.
5
Switch to Agent view
In Antigravity, click "Agent Manager" (robot icon). This is the asynchronous agent interface where you assign missions.
6
Choose model
Use Gemini 3 Pro (Planning) for architecture and complex logic tasks. Use Gemini 3 Fast for UI and CSS tasks. Claude Sonnet available as alternative.

Mission 5 — PDF generation (Week 3)
PASTE THIS INTO ANTIGRAVITY AGENT MANAGER — MISSION 5
Read AGENTS.md. Create js/pdf.js using jsPDF from CDN (https://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/jspdf/2.5.1/jspdf.umd.min.js).

Implement four PDF generators. Each returns a blob URL and uploads to Supabase Storage. Each PDF must include: Kasper Vendor OS branding top-left, vendor company name and logo top-right, TRN number, document reference number, creation date.
