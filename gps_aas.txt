Overview
We start with phone-based GPS from day one — it is free and works for 80% of use cases. Once we have a vendor base from the marketplace and Vendor OS, we introduce Teltonika FMB920 hardware GPS devices on vendor trucks at AED 80–150/device/month. Hardware pays back in under 3 months. Once the device is on the truck, the vendor cannot leave — it is physically on their asset and locked to our platform. That is the switching cost that builds our moat.
The two-phase GPS strategy
Phase 1 (live now): phone-based GPS. Driver opens job link on their phone. Browser pushes coordinates every 10 seconds via gps.js to Supabase. Free. Zero hardware. Works for freight trucks in 80% of cases — this is what we use in the marketplace from day one.  Phase 3 (Month 6+): Teltonika FMB920 hardware installed on vendor truck or equipment asset. Hardwired to ignition. Sends GPS, engine hours, fuel, and ignition status every 10–30 seconds regardless of phone. Kasper charges AED 80–150/device/month. Vendor subscription pays back hardware in under 3 months.
The Problem — Why Phone GPS Alone is Not Enough
🔋  Phone GPS dies mid-trip
Driver's battery hits 5%. Screen turns off. GPS stops. Client's tracking map goes blank. The vendor has no idea where their asset is. This happens on every third job in real conditions.
🚜  Equipment has no phone
A crane, generator, or compressor rented for 3 months on a construction site does not have a phone attached. Phone GPS works for trucks. It cannot work for equipment assets on long-term hire.
🏗️  Enterprise clients need hardware
A main contractor managing AED 50M of equipment across 12 sites will not accept a driver's phone as the tracking solution. Hardware GPS is the threshold for enterprise contracts and serious procurement.
⛽  No fuel or engine data from phones
Phone GPS gives coordinates only. Vendors have no fuel consumption, engine hours, or idling time data. They cannot predict maintenance. Breakdowns cost AED 30–80K per incident — entirely preventable.
💸  Existing hardware is too expensive for SMEs
Fleet management systems in the UAE cost AED 3,000–8,000 per vehicle upfront plus high monthly fees. A 3-truck operator cannot afford this. Nobody has made GPS affordable for the SME fleet segment.
🔓  SaaS alone has no switching cost
A Vendor OS subscriber can cancel and move to a competitor with one click. A vendor with a Kasper GPS device on every truck cannot — the device is physically on their asset and locked to our platform.

Why We Build This
Physical switching cost: a GPS device installed on a truck is the strongest retention mechanism we have. A vendor cannot leave the platform without physically removing the device and losing all historical data.
Recurring revenue: AED 80–150/device/month is independent of marketplace job volume. It continues whether or not the vendor uses the marketplace that month.
Enterprise threshold: hardware GPS is what large construction clients require before signing multi-month capacity contracts. Phone GPS is not sufficient for enterprise procurement.
Telematics foundation: every engine hour, every GPS ping from hardware devices builds the dataset that Product 4 (Telematics and Data Intelligence) runs on. GPS hardware is not optional — it is the data pipeline.
